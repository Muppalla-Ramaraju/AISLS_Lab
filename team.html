Laboratory Team

Meet our dedicated researchers advancing sustainable livestock systems through AI

Current Team Members

Dr. Karun Kaniyamattam

Lab Mentor & Assistant Professor

Dr. Kaniyamattam leads our laboratory's mission to develop artificial intelligence-based decision models for sustainable livestock systems. His expertise spans mathematical modeling, epidemiology, and economic systems analysis.

Adeolu Jose

Ph.D. Researcher

Adeolu specializes in leveraging artificial intelligence for sustainable livestock systems in my role as a Doctoral Researcher at Texas A&M University. My research focus is on developing advanced AI models and algorithms to address critical challenges in agricultural production systems, particularly Bovine Respiratory Disease (BRD) in US beef cattle systems. Additionally, I engage in cutting-edge research to create economic decision models for feedlot and veterinary syndromic surveillance systems.

Serin

Ph.D. Researcher

Serin is a Ph.D. researcher in Animal Science at Texas A&M University, where she uses modeling to improve the sustainability of livestock production systems. Her work integrates artificial intelligence, system dynamics, and economic analysis to evaluate strategies for reducing methane emissions and enhancing production efficiency. By leveraging data from precision livestock technologies, she supports informed decision-making in beef systems. Serin is also passionate about mentoring students and contributing to science-based agricultural policy.

Megha

Researcher

Megha Poyyara Saiju (DVM, MVSc, PhD, PGTMD) is a veterinary science researcher specialised in nutritional physiology and functional food production. Her research work explored the estrogenic impact of soybean isoflavones on ovarian receptor dynamics in layer chickens. She also pioneered a safe and effective method to produce vitamin D3- and zinc-enriched designer eggs. She has over five years of experience in analytical and molecular techniques from a NABL-accredited Central Instruments Laboratory, and is currently contributing to multidisciplinary research initiatives at the Artificial Intelligence for Sustainable Livestock Systems Lab at Texas A&M University
